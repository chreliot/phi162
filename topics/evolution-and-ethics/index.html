Topic 3: Evolution and Ethics

Reading

  1. Darwin, Charles. 1871. Descent of Man, or, Selection in Relation to Sex. short excerpt distributed in class.
  2. Wilson, E. O. 1975. Sociobiology: The New Synthesis. short excerpt distributed in class.
  3. Kitcher, Philip. 1993. “Four Ways of ‘Biologicizing’ Ethics.” In In Mendel’s mirror: philosophical reflections on biology. Oxford University Press, 2003. Find the eBook through Axinn Library here.
  4. Godfrey-Smith, Peter. 2013. “Evolution and Social Behavior,” Chapter 8 of Philosophy of Biology, Princeton University Press. via Axinn Library (Hofstra login required). Please note: This eBook currently has “Limited User Access (1 Copies Available),” so please consider downloading the chapter as PDF and disconnecting from the eBook. That way other students will not be prevented from downloading it. (Once many of us download it, the library will probably automatically increase the access level to “unlimited.”) If you have problems accessing it, email me. The librarians have now upgraded this book to unlimited access.

Reading questions

  • What does Wilson think the relationship between philosophical ethics and evolutionary biology should be?
  • What reasons does he offer in favor of that relationship?
  • What four kinds of relationships between evolutionary biology and philosophical ethics does Kitcher think might be embraced by someone like Wilson?
  • What arguments does Kitcher offer for, or against each of them? Which are viable, in his opinion?
  • What are the three main approaches to cooperation that were popular during the twentieth century, as described by Godfrey-Smith?
  • How does the Prisoner’s Dilemma model work, and what conclusions might we draw about how the model itself works, just from analyzing it?
  • What assumptions might we make to connect the PD model to cooperation more generally?

See also

  • Radiolab. 2019. “Tit for Tat.” WNYC Studios. Podcast episode about cooperation and the Prisoner’s Dilemma.
  • Silk, Joan. 2013. “Reciprocal Altruism.” Current Biology 23(18): R827-R828.
  • Pennisi, Elisabeth. 2009. “On the Origin of Cooperation” Science 325 (5945): 1196-1199. DOI: 10.1126/science.325_1196.
  • Laland, Kevin N. 2017. “On the Origin of Cooperation.” New Atlantis.
