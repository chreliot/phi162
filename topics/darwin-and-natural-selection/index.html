Topic 2: Darwin and Natural Selection

Reading

  1. Darwin, Charles. 1859. The Origin of Species.
  • Read Chapter 3. Then, read just up to page 10 in Chapter 4, and finally the final paragraph of Chapter 14 (on the last page). Available via Blackboard.
  1. Waters, C. Kenneth. 2003. “The Arguments in the Origin of Species.” In  The Cambridge Companion to Darwin, edited by Gregory Radick and Jonathan Hodge, 116–139. Cambridge University Press. via Blackboard. The entire book is also available at Hofstra Axinn Library: QH31.D2 C185 2003.

Reading questions

  • What is “the struggle for existence,” and what different forms does it take?
  • What are the causes of there everywhere being struggles for existence?
  • When we look at nature and see abundance, what does Darwin insist we’re overlooking?
  • What is the Staffordshire heath example supposed to demonstrate?
  • What is the clover/humble-bee example supposed to demonstrate?
