Topic 7: Health and Disease

Reading

  1. Cooper, Rachel. 2002. “Disease.” Studies in History and Philosophy of Science Part C: Studies in History and Philosophy of Biological and Biomedical Sciences. 33(2):263–28.2 https://doi.org/10.1016/S0039-3681(02)00018-3 (or, if you have a problem accessing through that the DOI, try this Hofstra-library link: https://hofstra.on.worldcat.org/oclc/4931258279).
  2. Griffiths, Paul E. and John Matthewson. 2018. “Evolution, Dysfunction, and Disease: A Reappraisal.” The British Journal for the Philosophy of Science 69(2): 301–327. (PDF via Axinn Library). Focus on reading section 2 on the “selected effect account” and then look through the next few sections for replies to objections.

Reading questions

  • What is Boorse’s definition of “disease”?
  • How does Boorse patch up his use of “function,” and what problems does that introduce for his definition, according to Cooper?
  • What are the general problems Cooper identifies for “disease as disfunction” accounts?
  • What is Cooper’s alternative proposal for how to define “disease”?
  • What is a “selected effect” account?
  • What contested assumptions does a selected effect account depend on?
  • How do Griffiths and Matthewson support a selected effect account of disease in response to objections form normativists?

See also

  • “81 Words: The story of how the American Psychiatric Association decided in 1973 that homosexuality was no longer a mental illness.” This American Life. WBEZ Chicago.
  • Murphy, Dominic. 2020. “Concepts of Health and Disease”. Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy.
