Topic 1: Before Natural Selection

Reading

  1. Aristotle, excerpts from Physics and Parts of Animals (about 350 BCE). Handout. Available through Blackboard > Course Documents.
    • Note: This is short but dense. Try to work through it carefully; see the reading questions below ↓.
  2. William Smellie, “Of the Progressive Scale or Chain of Beings in the Universe” (1791). Available through Blackboard > Course Documents.
    • Note: This is an account of the relationships among all living things from the perspective of late-eighteenth century biology in England. It’s deeply racist; that’s part of the picture.
  3. Philip Sloan, “Evolutionary Thought before Darwin” (2019) in the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy.” Read sections 1.1 and 4.1. Open access online.
    • Note: This account includes some historical detail which you may want to look through more quickly. Focus on picking out what the several main ideas are in each section.
    • Note: An important word in this reading is “teleological.” Read this as “having to do with purpose” and synonymous with Aristotle’s “final cause” we discussed.

Reading questions

  • If you were Aristotle, or one of his followers, how would you explain what a hammer is, in terms of what makes it what it is?
  • If you were Aristotle, or one of his followers, how would you explain what an eagle is, in terms of what makes it what it is?
  • Try to make a list of some of Smellie’s claims about the relationships among living things.
  • Does Smellie offer arguments here for any of his claims?
  • (In Sloan) What is Plato’s picture of the universe in Timaeus as it relates to an account of the diversity of living things?
  • (In Sloan) What is Aristotle’s account of the development of organisms?
  • (In Sloan) What account of “species” does Aristotle’s account of development give rise to?
  • (In Sloan) What is Lamarck’s theory of species “transformism”?
  • (In Sloan) What was shared and different between St. Hilaire’s and Cuvier’s accounts of evolution?
