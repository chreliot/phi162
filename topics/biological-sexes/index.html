Topic 4: Biological Sexes

Reading

  1. Griffiths, Paul. 2020. “Sex is Real.” Aeon.
  2. Richardson, Sarah S. 2022. “Sex Contextualism.” Philosophy, Theory, and Practice in Biology 14 (2). doi: https://doi.org/10.3998/ptpbio.2096

Reading questions

  • What conceptions of sex does Griffiths argue against?
  • What conception of sex does Griffiths argue for?
  • What is Griffiths’s argument for his conception of sex?
  • Why are sex chromosomes not sufficient for biological sex?
  • How does Griffiths argue that his conception of biological sex cannot be used to characterize sex in non-biological human institutions?
  • What position on sex is Richardson most concerned to argue against?
  • What is Richardson’s “sex contextualism,” and what does it apply to?

See also

  • Richardson, Sarah S. 2015. “Sex, Gender, and the Human Genome.” Presentation at University of Pennsylvania, Penn Humanities Forum on Sex. video on Vimeo.
