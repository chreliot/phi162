About

This site is for the PHI 161 Philosophy of Science course (Fall 2021) at Hofstra University.

In addition to this site, we will also use a Blackboard site. Blackboard is available through the password-protected Hofstra Portal. Why use both? This site is easy to access and to publish to; the Blackboard site can present password-protected information. Material in copyright and grades will be available through Blackboard. There is a link to Blackboard at the top of this (and every) page ↑.

Structure of this site

What you’re responsible for each day is listed on the schedule on the front page by date. The schedule links to:

  • Topics pages, normally spanning several class sessions. They list Required reading assignments, with details about how to find them, as well as further, not-required content you may be interested in under See also. Afternotes, if any, refer to follow-up items from class, things students send me, and similar.

  • Assignments pages describing tasks you have to complete and submit.

  • Tests pages gradually accumulating test questions for your first test (= “midterm exam”) and second test (= “final exam”). As there are two tests, there will be a list of possible questions for each one.

  • A Resources page with other sources connected with the topics of this course.

  • Notes pages containing anything else I want to share with you.

Besides finding what you need from the schedule, you can access lists of each of these kinds of content through the buttons at the top.

Share things

Think of anything that reminds you of what we’re talking about in the course? Students sometimes send me things they think others might enjoy seeing. It’s straightforward for me to add such things to the topics pages.
